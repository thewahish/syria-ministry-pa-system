üö® MAJOR WEAKNESSES OF THIS PACKAGE

1. Venue Coverage Limitations:
  • Designed for 800-1000 people MAX, not 2000 as originally required
  • Rear 30% of large venue may have poor audio quality
  • No line array precision - uneven SPL distribution
2. Wireless System Compromises:
  • Operating range only 50-80m (vs 150m requirement)
  • Analog wireless = more noise, less clarity
  • Limited frequency coordination (interference risk)
  • 4-6 hour battery life (may need mid-event battery change)
3. Professional Appearance Issues:
  • Multiple speaker stands visible (not clean line array look)
  • Budget equipment appearance may not suit government dignity
  • More complex setup with multiple connection points
4. Reliability Concerns:
  • Budget equipment = higher failure risk during events
  • Limited warranty coverage (1-2 years vs 5-6 years)
  • Less robust construction for frequent transport
5. Future Limitations:
  • Difficult to expand system capacity later
  • Limited upgrade path without complete replacement
  • May need full replacement in 3-5 years vs 10+ years for premium
6. Operational Challenges:
  • More complex setup requiring multiple power outlets
  • Higher operator skill required (more things to manage)
  • Limited remote control capabilities

üõ°Ô∏è MITIGATION STRATEGIES

How to Make This Package Work Better:

1. Venue Optimization:

  • Reduce Capacity: Use for 800-1000 person events maximum
  • Acoustic Treatment: Choose venues with good natural acoustics
  • Seating Layout: Concentrate audience in front 70% of venue

2. Operational Improvements:

  • Dual Operators: Train 2 people to manage the more complex setup
  • Pre-Event Testing: Always test all wireless frequencies before events
  • Backup Equipment: Keep spare batteries, cables, basic microphones

3. Phased Upgrade Path:

  • Year 1: Use budget system to establish need and experience
  • Year 2-3: Upgrade wireless system to digital (keep speakers)
  • Year 4-5: Upgrade to line array when budget allows
